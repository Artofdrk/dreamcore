INTRODUCTION

What are Liminal Spaces?

Liminal spaces are the threshold — the physical and emotional spaces between one destination and the next. They are the hallways, waiting rooms, abandoned malls, and empty indoor pools.

Stripped of their intended purpose and the people who usually inhabit them, these environments evoke an uncanny sense of familiarity, leaving you feeling as though you have walked these halls before in a distant, forgotten dream.

EXHIBIT I

Level 94 - "Motion"

Level 94 is a surreal, grainy 1930s-style town surrounded by infinite grass hills and a floating castle. During the day, the town is safe and empty, with vintage houses, cars, and a fountain of Almond Water. At night, the music stops and hostile “Animations” and other entities appear and attack. The surrounding hills are endless and glitch-like, containing random objects and fragments of buildings, with a water tower and a castle floating above on a transparent mountain.

EXHIBIT II

Level 37: "Sublimity"

Level 37, or the Poolrooms, is a vast maze of white tiled rooms filled with shallow, lukewarm water. Its architecture is irregular and surreal, with random pools, pillars, and staircases leading into deeper, dangerous areas. The water has calming effects from Epsom salt and unknown compounds, while sound is distorted and often muted, creating a quiet, eerie atmosphere with constant subtle ripples across all surfaces.

EXHIBIT III

Level 0: "The Lobby"

Level 0 is an endless, shifting maze of yellowed backrooms with damp carpets and flickering fluorescent lights. Its layout constantly changes, making it easy to get lost and disoriented. A loud buzzing from the lights causes headaches and mental strain, while the main dangers are starvation, dehydration, and confusion. Above the ceiling is a cramped, dusty space filled with wires, but it’s not safe to explore.
